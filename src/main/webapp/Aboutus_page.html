About Us

At Blood Bank, we are dedicated to saving lives through the generous donation of blood. Our mission is to provide a safe and reliable supply of blood products to hospitals and medical facilities throughout the region.

What is blood banking?

Blood banking is the process that takes place in the lab to make sure that donated blood, or blood products, are safe before they are used in blood transfusions and other medical procedures. Blood banking includes typing the blood for transfusion and testing for infectious diseases.

why private blood bank is important?

Private blood banks are important because they increase the supply of blood available for patients, provide greater convenience and have more rigorous screening and testing procedures to ensure the safety of donated blood.

what are tests take in blood bank?

Blood banks typically perform several tests to ensure the safety and compatibility of donated blood, including:
1. Blood typing and crossmatching to determine the blood group and identify any potential incompatibilities.
2. Screening for infectious diseases such as HIV, hepatitis B and C, syphilis, and West Nile virus.
3. Testing for antibodies to ensure that the donor is not at risk for transfusion-transmitted infections.
4. Performing hemoglobin and hematocrit tests to assess the donor's red blood cell count and overall health.
